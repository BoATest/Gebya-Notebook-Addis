1. THE PROBLEM — What Exactly Are We Solving?

Ethiopian shop owners are stuck between paper and tools not built for them. That gap is real. But we must be precise.


Questions:


What is the single biggest pain point? Is it:

Knowing how much profit they actually made today?
Remembering who owes them money (credit/debt tracking)?
Tracking inventory so they know when to restock?
Recording sales so they can compare months?
Something else entirely?
When a shop owner finishes a day, what is the ONE question they wish they could answer easily?

What happens when a shop owner loses their paper notebook? What is the real consequence?

Are most sales cash-based, or is there significant credit (buy now, pay later) in Ethiopian retail?



2. THE USER — Who Are We Building For?

This changes everything about design, language, features.


Questions:


What is the typical shop? A small kiosk? A medium-sized store? A clothing shop? A grocery?

What language does the user think in? Amharic? Oromo? English? Mixed? Does the app need full Amharic support or is English acceptable?

What phone do they use? Android? What price range? Is it a smartphone with good internet, or a basic Android with limited storage and slow 3G?

How tech-comfortable are they? Can they install an app from Play Store? Do they already use Telegram confidently? Do they understand "login" and "password"?

How many transactions does a typical shop have per day? 10? 50? 200?

Do they have employees who also need to record sales, or is it always one person?



3. THE PRODUCT — What Are We Actually Building?

This is where vision meets execution.


Questions:


Is this a web app, a mobile app, a Telegram bot, or a Progressive Web App (PWA)? Since you are using Replit, are you building a web-based tool that opens in a browser?

What is the ONE core screen? If the user opens the app and sees only one thing, what should it be?

What is the minimum feature set for v1? Can you list exactly 3–5 features and nothing more?

How does data entry work? Does the user type product names every time, or do they set up products once and then just tap to record a sale?

How do you handle Ethiopian Birr (ETB) formatting? Currency, number formatting?

Is offline support important? Many Ethiopian shops have unreliable internet. Can the app work without a connection and sync later?



4. THE CODE — How Is It Built on Replit?

To help you architect this properly.


Questions:


What language/framework are you using? Python (Flask/Django)? JavaScript (Express/React)? HTML/CSS/JS only?

Are you using a database? Which one? SQLite? PostgreSQL? Firebase? Or just storing data in a file or browser localStorage?

How is user data stored right now? Is there a concept of accounts/login, or does everyone share one view?

Is there a backend API, or is it all frontend?

What is the current file/folder structure? Can you share a rough listing?

Are you using any libraries or frameworks for the UI? Tailwind? Bootstrap? Or custom CSS?



5. THE ENGAGEMENT — How Do We Make Them Come Back?

The hardest problem in tools for non-technical users is retention.


Questions:


What will make a shop owner open this app EVERY DAY instead of reaching for their notebook?

What is the "aha moment"? The first time they see value. Is it seeing a daily total? Seeing who owes them money?

How do you handle the setup cost? Entering all existing products takes time. How do you make onboarding take less than 2 minutes?

Do you plan to send reminders? For example, "You have 3 customers who owe you money" or "You sold less today than yesterday"?

Is there a social or trust element? Ethiopian business culture is relationship-heavy. Does the tool acknowledge that?



6. THE FUTURE — Where Does This Go?

Thinking ahead shapes today's architecture.


Questions:


Do you envision multiple shops using this, or is it one-shop-at-a-time?

Could this eventually connect to mobile money (TeleBirr, M-Pesa)?

Would you want to add inventory management later?

Is there a possibility of a simple receipt/invoice feature? Print or share via WhatsApp/Telegram?

Would you ever want a dashboard that shows monthly trends, best-selling products, etc.?



7. THE CULTURAL CONTEXT — Ethiopian Trade Realities

Research-informed understanding.


Questions:


Do Ethiopian shop owners commonly give goods on credit (ደ Câm)? Is tracking debts a major pain?

Do they buy inventory from wholesalers and need to track costs vs selling prices?

Is there a common way Ethiopian shop owners calculate profit, or do many just "feel" whether they are doing well?

Do they operate on a calendar system? Ethiopian calendar (EC) vs Gregorian? This matters for date handling in the app.

Are there common product categories? (Merkato-style goods, groceries, clothing, electronics parts...) Does the app need category templates?
